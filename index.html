How we met

Our story begins in the heart of our village, where life moves at a simple, steady pace. We grew up just a few houses apart, yet our paths never really crossed until fate decided to bring us together through family. It was one of those sunny afternoons when we first saw each other, at a family gathering. Our parents had known each other for years, but it was that moment—when our eyes met for the first time—that something truly magical began to stir between us. From the moment we met, I knew you were the one I was meant to spend my life with, Jyoti. You are my heart, my home, and my forever. And today, as I take your hand in marriage, I am more certain than ever that our love story is just beginning.

20th November 2024

Lagan Puja and Ghidhari 10AM-5PM

Lagan Puja (also known as Lagna Puja or Lagna Sankalp) is an important religious ceremony in many Indian weddings, particularly in Hindu traditions. It marks the auspicious beginning of the marriage rituals and is often performed before the wedding day. The purpose of this puja is to invoke the blessings of the gods and to ensure that the wedding is conducted with the best possible spiritual and emotional intentions.

23th November

Haldi 10AM-11AM

The haldi ceremony is usually held separately for the bride and the groom. This one is for the bride. It is a low-key event that involves the application of turmeric paste on the hands, legs and face of the bride. The groom is not allowed to see the bride after this ritual until the final wedding ceremony starts.

Sangeet Night 8PM-12AM

This musical night is a celebration of the union of not only the couple but the bonding of both families. It is here that both sides bring down their barriers and mingle in a fun environment. It is a celebration that can be as lavish or as toned down as the families can afford. It’s usually held a day before the wedding. It may be held in a huge hotel room to accommodate all wedding guests, or it may be a small affair at the bride’s home. In some events men and women get separated due to their interests, for example men –booze, women- henna, younsters-dance. But most of the sangeet parties are huge and loud and every bit like a reception but super fun.

24th November

Baraat 7PM-12AM

Baarat prasthan is one of the joyful rituals of Bihari wedding. The groom leaves for the wedding venue in a beautifully decorated wedding car. The groom is followed by his friends and family in the wedding procession. After reaching the venue the groom is welcomed by his mother-in-law with a beautiful aarti.

Jaimala 8PM-12AM

The Jaimala ceremony is one of the fun-filled wedding rituals in Bihar. In this ritual both the Bihari couple exchange garlands at their wedding mandap. After exchanging the wedding garlands the bride’s mother burns a betel leaf into ashes. This wedding ritual is called Galsedi. The ashes of a burnt leaf are then applied to the groom’s face to ward off evil eyes.

25th November

Subh Vivah 7PM-12AM

This is the main event in the entire wedding when the bride and the groom tie the knot. The groom comes with the barat and is greeted by the bride side with some dhol of course as maximum number of guests would be attending this event. This is when the couple circle the sacred fire and are finally declared husband and wife.

26th November

Chauthari 10AM-7PM

The bride wears the ' chundri- saree', given by her mother-in-law, which is generally of red cotton with golden gota (golden ribbon)border.Her head covered with her 'chunri', she sits down with her husband and parents in law to do the 'samapan of the 'satyanarayan- katha'. Today the 'punahuti'- the havan will be done. So as to say to thank the gods and ancestors for the blessings showered for such a pious yagya and to allow everything to happen without any obstacles. The holy thread tied to the wrist of the bride at the 'satyanarayan katha held at her place for the commencement of the wedding events, is now opened and dropped in the havan fire, signifying , that whatever was there of her 'peehar' is now a thing of the past . the punitji now ties her a new dhaga signifying, that now on wards, everything of this house now shall carry her bearings.
